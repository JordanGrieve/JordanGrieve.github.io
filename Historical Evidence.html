The Drake Equation

Firstly, the Drake equation is a problematic theory used to appraise the “number of active communicative extra-terrestrial civilizations in the Milky Way galaxy.” Devised by Frank Drake, an American astronomer and astrophysicist and member of SETI (search for extra-terrestrial intelligence). The Drake equation was written in 1961 for the purpose of a way to “stimulate scientific dialogue at the first scientific meeting on the search for extra-terrestrial intelligence”. Mr. Drake’s equation, The Drake has influenced the knowledge we have regarding the existence of extra-terrestrial life tremendously as the equation drew attention to some scientific problems related to life in the universe.

The Drawbacks of The Drake Equation

However, The Drake equation had its criticism as the equation relies mostly from the observation that terms in the equation is largely based on conjecture making some of the parameters not statistical possible. Results of this is that The Drake equation cannot be used as a conclusion regarding the existing of external life forms as the margin of error is far too big to be considered acceptable.
